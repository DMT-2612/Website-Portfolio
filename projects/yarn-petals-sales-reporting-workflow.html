Yarn Petals: Sales Reporting Workflow Redesign

An AI-assisted BA delivery project that turned fragmented sales records into a structured reporting workflow and Power BI dashboard.

Role: Business Analyst Client: Ivy / Yarn Petals Tools: AI tools, Google Colab, Python, Power BI, Excel

Project overview

Yarn Petals is an e-commerce crochet flower brand that needed a cleaner way to manage and review sales data across Shopify exports, manual order files, and social media orders. The project redesigned the reporting workflow so the owner could move from inconsistent source files to a single structured dataset and an interactive Power BI sales dashboard.

The work combined business analysis, requirements structuring, data workflow planning, AI-assisted technical execution, dashboard validation, and handover documentation. My role was to own the project direction, define the business rules, coordinate the solution flow, and use AI tools to accelerate the technical build while checking the outputs against the business requirements.

Business problem

The client had three years of sales activity spread across different formats. Shopify data was messy, manual 2025 orders did not match the structured reporting format, and Instagram and Facebook orders needed standardised order numbers and categories.

This created reporting risk for the ownership transition: financial totals could be misread, category analysis was inconsistent, and the owner had to rely on manual cleaning before making decisions.

My BA contribution

  • Clarified the reporting problem and translated it into business and functional requirements.
  • Defined the universal dataset structure needed for repeatable reporting.
  • Documented product, animal, colour, occasion, sales channel, and missing-value business rules.
  • Scoped what was included and excluded to keep the solution practical for a small business owner.
  • Used AI to support technical execution across Python logic, dashboard build iteration, and documentation.
  • Validated the final dashboard against expected totals, filters, privacy rules, and handover needs.

AI-assisted delivery approach

AI was used as a delivery accelerator for technical work, especially code exploration, Power BI structure, DAX guidance, report layout iteration, troubleshooting, and documentation drafting. The project was still led through BA judgement: I framed the problem, checked the logic, decided what belonged in scope, and reviewed whether the outputs matched the client workflow.

This showed an important practical skill: using AI not just to generate technical artifacts, but to break a messy business problem into clear requirements, structured tasks, validation checks, and a handover-ready solution.

Workflow redesign

  • Raw data inputs: Shopify CSV, cleaned Shopify file, manual order file, and cleaned order file.
  • Transformation layer: Python workflow in Google Colab for cleaning, mapping, and standardisation.
  • Structured output: `Universal.xlsx` as the single reporting source of truth.
  • Reporting layer: Power BI project using Orders, DimDate, Metrics, and a field parameter table.
  • Handover support: requirements document, dashboard plan, measure guide, and validation scripts.

Dashboard outcome

The final Power BI dashboard gives the owner a one-page view of sales performance with KPI cards, filters, channel analysis, monthly trend, state performance, and distribution views by colour, product, animal, and occasion. It supports switching between net sales and order count for the main charts while keeping the KPI cards stable.

Key validation targets included net sales of $77,601.98 AUD, 1,132 distinct orders, and 744 total customers in the unfiltered dataset. Customer email data remained in the model for operational use but was excluded from report visuals.
