# ICMRA Fundraising Analytics Dashboard

## Project Snapshot

This portfolio project presents a strategic Business Intelligence solution for the International Consortium for Medical Research Advancement (ICMRA), a medical research fundraising organisation operating across multiple contributor groups, campaign types, regions, and referral channels.

The project was designed to support senior management decision-making by transforming raw fundraising activity from 2019 to 2025 into an executive Power BI dashboard, advanced analytics model, and board-facing infographic.

## Business Problem

ICMRA needed more than a static fundraising report. Senior leaders required a clear view of:

- Whether fundraising performance was growing sustainably.
- Which contributor groups created the most value.
- Which campaigns produced the strongest return against target and budget.
- Where contributor retention risk and dormant revenue existed.
- What growth levers could help close future revenue gaps.

The dashboard was built as a decision-support tool for campaign optimisation, contributor strategy, retention planning, and forward revenue scenario analysis.

## Key Outcomes

- Cleaned the fact table from 45,000 pledge-payment rows to 44,993 valid records after duplicate removal.
- Imputed 92 missing payment dates using campaign-level median payment lag logic while retaining an audit flag.
- Corrected malformed account IDs and category inconsistencies to improve relationship accuracy and visual reliability.
- Built an analytics framework covering executive KPIs, contributor segmentation, campaign performance, RFM, cohort retention, CLV proxy, and scenario planning.
- Identified that revenue peaked at $12.81M in 2023 before falling by 78.3% to $2.79M in 2024, highlighting the risk of treating peak performance as a stable baseline.
- Identified $19.03M in reactivated revenue from 1,353 contributors who returned after more than 365 days of inactivity.
- Identified approximately $6.3M in dormant contributor value still exposed to retention risk.
- Showed that a +20% revenue target could be approached through a balanced +6.27% uplift across average pledge, giving frequency, and retention drivers.
